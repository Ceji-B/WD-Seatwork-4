Vigan City

A UNESCO World Heritage site located in the Ilocos Sur province of the Philippines. Renowned for its well-preserved Spanish colonial and Asian architecture, it features cobblestone streets, historic buildings, and horse-drawn carriages known as kalesa. Vigan offers a unique glimpse into the Philippines' colonial past with attractions such as Calle Crisologo, Bantay Bell Tower, and Baluarte. Explore the city's rich history, culture, and cuisine on a guided tour with TRAVI | TRAVELS.

Tour Destinations

Calle Crisologo

Calle Crisologo is the most famous street in Vigan, known for its preserved Spanish colonial and Asian architecture. Walking down this cobblestone street lined with ancestral houses, antique shops, and cafes, you can experience the charm of a bygone era.

Bantay Bell Tower

The Bantay Bell Tower is a historic watchtower and church belfry that offers panoramic views of Vigan and its surrounding areas. This iconic structure played a significant role during wartime and remains a testament to the city's resilience and history.

Baluarte

Baluarte is a sprawling zoological park owned by local politician Chavit Singson. It features a variety of animals, a butterfly garden, and a safari gallery. Visitors can enjoy close encounters with the animals and even ride horses and camels.
